The {HOOK} bank — one-liners to slot in

For the {HOOK} placeholder, pick one of these one-liners that fits the company, OR write a fresh one. The goal is to prove you actually read their site or job post, not to flatter them.

Generic hooks (safe defaults when you do not know the company):

  • I am drawn to teams that ship fast and trust their engineers to own decisions end to end, and the role description suggests {COMPANY} is one of them.
  • The {ROLE} responsibilities map closely to what I have already shipped alone on Plotzy, and I would like to do that kind of work as part of a team that already moves at speed.

Company-specific hook templates (replace the bracketed parts):

  • I have been [using / following / reading the changelog of] {COMPANY} for [time period] and I appreciate that you [specific decision you noticed: shipped X, open-sourced Y, wrote about Z].
  • I noticed {COMPANY} uses [specific tech they mentioned] in your stack; I have shipped that on Plotzy and would enjoy working on it inside a larger codebase.
  • Your post mentions [specific responsibility]; I implemented exactly that on Plotzy in [specific subsystem]. Happy to walk through the decision tree on a call.

For AI companies specifically:

  • I have read your [docs / blog post about X] and the way {COMPANY} thinks about [specific concept] mirrors how I built [related subsystem in Plotzy]. I would like to learn more by joining you.

For dev tool companies (Vercel, Supabase, Resend, etc.):

  • I am already a user of {COMPANY} in production on Plotzy. Joining the team that ships the product I rely on every day would be the obvious next step.

TIER 4 — Cold outreach (for after week 2)

When you have applied to 100+ jobs through the boards above, start reaching out cold via LinkedIn or email to specific engineering managers at smaller companies.

Template message for cold outreach (LinkedIn DM, 280 chars max):

Hi {NAME}, I built a full-stack bilingual SaaS solo this year
(plotzy.co, 100K lines of TypeScript, AI + audiobook). Saw {COMPANY}
is shipping {SOMETHING}. Are you taking applications for full-stack
engineers? Happy to share the repo. Faris

Find targets: - Open LinkedIn -> Search "Engineering Manager" + "{Tech you know}". - Filter by company size 11 to 200. - Filter by location (any). - Send 20 DMs per day for 5 days. Expect 1 to 3 conversations.

What to NOT apply to (waste of time)

  • Roles asking for "5 to 7 years of experience". Below 3 years is fine to stretch. 5+ is not.
  • Roles requiring an EU or US work permit upfront. If they say "must have right to work in the EU/US", skip. Apply only if they say "open to sponsor" or "remote anywhere".
  • Roles at consulting agencies (Accenture, Tata, Cognizant). Low pay, long hours, hard to leave once you join.
  • Roles that require a degree in Computer Science specifically. Your degree is BIT and that is fine for 95% of dev roles, but explicit CS requirements are usually rigid HR filters that the recruiter cannot override.

By end of week one, you should have 20 to 30 active applications. The funnel from there: ~10% reply, ~3% interview, ~1% offer. Twenty applications -> two replies, one interview. Two hundred applications -> twenty replies, six interviews, two offers.

Chapter 3, Application Tracker

Application Tracker — Faris Hamdan

One row per application. Update every time you apply, every time you hear back, every interview. Treat this as the single source of truth for the job search.

Status legend:

  • 🟢 applied — submitted, waiting for response
  • 🟡 contacted — recruiter or hiring manager replied
  • 🔵 screening — assessment, take-home, or first call scheduled
  • 🟣 interview — onsite (remote) panel scheduled
  • 🟠 offer — offer on the table
  • 🔴 rejected — declined, archived
  • ⚪ ghosted — 14+ days no reply, treat as rejected

How to fill in each column

  • Date: YYYY-MM-DD so it sorts.
  • Company: official name (not the careers page URL).
  • Role: exactly as the posting calls it (Software Engineer II, not Software Engineer).
  • Source: where you found the job (Wellfound, Remote OK, YC, direct, referral, cold DM).
  • Salary: if posted, write $4K/mo or $50K/yr. If not, write not posted.
  • CL Variant: which cover letter variant you sent (A, B, or C from 01_COVER_LETTERS.md).
  • Status: the emoji + word.
  • Next step: concrete action with a date. If "wait for reply", write Follow up YYYY-MM-DD for 5 business days out.

Tips

  • Apply within 24 hours of posting. Jobs older than 14 days are often already filled with a frontrunner.
  • Personalise one sentence per company. Recruiters smell mass copy-paste in under 3 seconds.
  • Track LinkedIn message threads here too, with Source: LinkedIn.
  • Update status every Friday so the weekly summary stays honest.

When to fire-and-forget vs really invest

  • Tier 3 board jobs: fire-and-forget. Three minutes max per app.
  • Tier 1 direct applications: invest 15 to 20 minutes per app to read the company blog and write one specific paragraph.
  • YC startups: invest 10 minutes per app, read the company page on YC and mention what you noticed.
  • Tier 2 agencies (Toptal, Turing, Arc): 30 to 60 minutes for the initial application + screening prep.

When to follow up

Days since application Action
5 business days Send a short follow-up (template in 01_COVER_LETTERS.md)
10 business days One more follow-up if the role is still posted
14 business days, no reply Mark as ⚪ ghosted, move to Archive

Do not chase further. Energy goes to the next 25 applications.

How to apply fast (90 seconds per Tier 3 application)

The board applications should be quick. Here is the muscle memory:

  1. Open the job posting in a new tab. Scan it for 15 seconds. Decide yes or no.
  2. If yes, open 01_COVER_LETTERS.md. Copy the right variant (A for AI, B for startup, C for established).
  3. Open the company website (about page or blog) for 30 seconds. Find one specific thing to reference in {HOOK}.
  4. Paste the variant into the application form. Replace {COMPANY}, {ROLE}, and {HOOK}. Total 20 seconds.
  5. Attach docs/Faris_Hamdan_CV.pdf and submit. 10 seconds.
  6. Log in 03_TRACKER.md. 15 seconds.

Total: 90 seconds.

10 applications per day at 90 seconds each = 15 minutes per day.

How to prep for the first call (30 minutes)

When you have an interview scheduled, do this prep:

  1. 10 minutes — Read the company's homepage, blog, and "About" page. Note: the founders, the funding round if any, the current team size, the main product, and the most recent blog post.

  2. 10 minutes — Read the job posting again. Write down: - Three things the role needs that you have done on Plotzy. - One thing the role needs that you have not done. Plan how to frame it: "I have not done X specifically, but I built Y which is the same shape; I can ramp in a week."

  3. 10 minutes — Plan your answers to the two questions every first call asks: - "Tell me about yourself." (60-second answer, mention Plotzy by sentence 2.) - "Why us?" (30-second answer, name one specific thing about the company you read in step 1.)

That is it. Do not over-prepare. The first call is 30 to 45 minutes of mutual sniff-testing. Confidence + Plotzy + the right energy wins it.

Common reasons people quit before the offer comes

Read these once, then read them again whenever you want to give up.

  1. The silence is louder than the rejections. 80% of applications are ghosted. This is normal, not a signal about you.
  2. Two months of work is the baseline, not the exception. Anyone who got hired in week 2 won the lottery. Plan for week 6.
  3. The board jobs feel demoralising because they are. That is why Tier 1 direct applications and YC are higher signal. Spend more time there if you are losing energy.
  4. One offer breaks the entire dam. The moment you get one credible offer, the recruiters who ghosted you suddenly reply. Stay the course.

Open 03_TRACKER.md. Fill in the weekly summary row. Then ask yourself three questions:

  1. Did I send 25 applications this week? If no, what got in the way?
  2. What was the reply rate? If under 5%, the cover letter or the CV needs work. Tell Claude, we will revise.
  3. What was the most interesting role I saw? Apply to two more like it next week.

That is the loop. Run it. The offers come.
