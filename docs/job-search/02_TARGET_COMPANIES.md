## TIER 1B — Y Combinator startups (use workatastartup.com)

Y Combinator's job board is the highest signal-to-noise ratio for AI and
SaaS startups hiring globally. Most YC startups are tiny (5 to 50 people)
and hire fast.

**Single best move**: create one account at workatastartup.com, set your
preferences, and let the algorithm match you. You will get 5 to 15 fresh
matches per week.

Direct URL: https://www.workatastartup.com

Filter by:
- Role: Full-Stack Engineer, Software Engineer
- Remote: Yes
- Experience: All (do not exclude entry-level, many startups want
  juniors with portfolios)
- Visa sponsorship: Yes (also include "Open to relocate")

When you find a match, apply directly through the YC interface. The
founder sees your application within hours, not weeks.

## TIER 4 — Cold outreach (for after week 2)

When you have applied to 100+ jobs through the boards above, start
reaching out cold via LinkedIn or email to specific engineering
managers at smaller companies.

Template message for cold outreach (LinkedIn DM, 280 chars max):

```
Hi {NAME}, I built a full-stack bilingual SaaS solo this year
(plotzy.co, 100K lines of TypeScript, AI + audiobook). Saw {COMPANY}
is shipping {SOMETHING}. Are you taking applications for full-stack
engineers? Happy to share the repo. Faris
```

Find targets:
- Open LinkedIn -> Search "Engineering Manager" + "{Tech you know}".
- Filter by company size 11 to 200.
- Filter by location (any).
- Send 20 DMs per day for 5 days. Expect 1 to 3 conversations.

## What to NOT apply to (waste of time)

- Roles asking for "5 to 7 years of experience". Below 3 years is fine
  to stretch. 5+ is not.
- Roles requiring an EU or US work permit upfront. If they say "must
  have right to work in the EU/US", skip. Apply only if they say
  "open to sponsor" or "remote anywhere".
- Roles at consulting agencies (Accenture, Tata, Cognizant). Low pay,
  long hours, hard to leave once you join.
- Roles that require a degree in Computer Science specifically. Your
  degree is BIT and that is fine for 95% of dev roles, but explicit CS
  requirements are usually rigid HR filters that the recruiter cannot
  override.

## Action checklist for week one

- [ ] Create accounts on the four required platforms:
  - [ ] Wellfound (15 min)
  - [ ] Y Combinator Work at a Startup (10 min)
  - [ ] Indeed (5 min)
  - [ ] Toptal application (the test will come later, just submit)
- [ ] Apply to 5 Tier 1 companies directly (use Variant C, customise
      one sentence per company).
- [ ] Apply to 5 YC startups via Work at a Startup (use Variant B).
- [ ] Apply to 10 Tier 3 board jobs (use Variant B for startups, C for

By end of week one, you should have **20 to 30 active applications**.
The funnel from there: ~10% reply, ~3% interview, ~1% offer. Twenty
applications -> two replies, one interview. Two hundred applications
-> twenty replies, six interviews, two offers.
