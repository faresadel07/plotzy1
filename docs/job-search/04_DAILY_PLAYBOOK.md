## How to apply fast (90 seconds per Tier 3 application)

The board applications should be quick. Here is the muscle memory:

1. Open the job posting in a new tab. Scan it for 15 seconds. Decide
   yes or no.
2. If yes, open `01_COVER_LETTERS.md`. Copy the right variant (A for AI,
   B for startup, C for established).
3. Open the company website (about page or blog) for 30 seconds. Find
   one specific thing to reference in {HOOK}.
4. Paste the variant into the application form. Replace {COMPANY},
   {ROLE}, and {HOOK}. Total 20 seconds.
5. Attach `docs/Faris_Hamdan_CV.pdf` and submit. 10 seconds.
6. Log in `03_TRACKER.md`. 15 seconds.

Total: 90 seconds.

10 applications per day at 90 seconds each = 15 minutes per day.

## How to handle the first reply

When a recruiter or hiring manager messages you, the reply window is
4 to 12 hours. Do not let it go past 24 hours.

Template reply (positive, available, asking for time):

```
Hi {NAME},

Thank you for reaching out about the {ROLE} at {COMPANY}. I am
available to discuss this week. Tuesday and Thursday afternoon
(Amman time, UTC+3) work well for me. What time slots fit your
schedule?

You can find my CV here: https://github.com/faresadel07/plotzy1/raw/master/docs/Faris_Hamdan_CV.pdf
The live demo of Plotzy is at https://plotzy.co
The open source repo is at https://github.com/faresadel07/plotzy1

Looking forward to the conversation.

Faris Hamdan
```

If they ask for a coding challenge or take-home, accept and ask for
the deadline. Always finish a take-home within 48 hours, even if they
give you a week. Recruiters track turnaround time.

## How to prep for the first call (30 minutes)

When you have an interview scheduled, do this prep:

1. **10 minutes** — Read the company's homepage, blog, and "About" page.
   Note: the founders, the funding round if any, the current team
   size, the main product, and the most recent blog post.

2. **10 minutes** — Read the job posting again. Write down:
   - Three things the role needs that you have done on Plotzy.
   - One thing the role needs that you have not done. Plan how to
     frame it: "I have not done X specifically, but I built Y which
     is the same shape; I can ramp in a week."

3. **10 minutes** — Plan your answers to the two questions every
   first call asks:
   - "Tell me about yourself." (60-second answer, mention Plotzy by
     sentence 2.)
   - "Why us?" (30-second answer, name one specific thing about the
     company you read in step 1.)

That is it. Do not over-prepare. The first call is 30 to 45 minutes
of mutual sniff-testing. Confidence + Plotzy + the right energy wins
it.

## Common reasons people quit before the offer comes

Read these once, then read them again whenever you want to give up.

1. **The silence is louder than the rejections.** 80% of applications
   are ghosted. This is normal, not a signal about you.
2. **Two months of work is the baseline, not the exception.** Anyone
   who got hired in week 2 won the lottery. Plan for week 6.
3. **The board jobs feel demoralising because they are.** That is why
   Tier 1 direct applications and YC are higher signal. Spend more
   time there if you are losing energy.
4. **One offer breaks the entire dam.** The moment you get one
   credible offer, the recruiters who ghosted you suddenly reply.
   Stay the course.

## End of every Friday: the weekly review

Open `03_TRACKER.md`. Fill in the weekly summary row. Then ask
yourself three questions:

1. **Did I send 25 applications this week?** If no, what got in the way?
2. **What was the reply rate?** If under 5%, the cover letter or the
   CV needs work. Tell Claude, we will revise.
3. **What was the most interesting role I saw?** Apply to two more
   like it next week.

That is the loop. Run it. The offers come.
